Why does coral bleaching happen?

Coral colonies consist of thousands of tiny animals called polyps that capture their prey using stinging tentacles. Lead scientist Vollmer says reef-building corals form a special symbiosis with photosynthetic algae that provide the coral animal with food in the form of sugars. The symbiotic relationship can doom coral subject to thermal bleaching, when rising water temperatures stress the coral so much it expels the nutrient-providing algae from its tissues, causing the coral to turn completely white.

Hard working PHD Student at Northeastern University

Witnessing disease outbreaks that have nearly annihilated staghorn coral colonies in the Caribbean, Northeastern scientist Steven Vollmer and his team identified dozens of staghorn genotypes from Florida and Panama that are resistant and highly resistant to disease. The best part? This isn't just wishful thinking. In some places, these genetic heroes are already making a comeback!

Whether the discovery translates into reefs designed for immunity remains to be seen — but Vollmer and us fish hope that will be the case!
